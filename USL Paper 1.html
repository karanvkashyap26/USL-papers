Clustering using K-Means

Segmentation using Age and Spending Score

In [74]:
'''Age and spending Score'''
X1 = df[['Age' , 'Spending Score (1-100)']].iloc[: , :].values
inertia = []
for n in range(1 , 11):
    algorithm = (KMeans(n_clusters = n ,init='k-means++', n_init = 10 ,max_iter=300, 
                        tol=0.0001,  random_state= 111  , algorithm='elkan') )
    algorithm.fit(X1)
    inertia.append(algorithm.inertia_)

Selecting N Clusters based in inertia(Squared distance between centroids and data points, should be less)

Conclusion:

We have converted the unstructured data into meaningful cluster using age, annual income and spending score. This information is very useful and it can generate high profit in business if this data is properly interpreted and promoted to the specific target group.

We can see 6 cluster in the above 3-Dimensional graph which is classified and spread accross the graph taking into consideraton age, annual income and spending score

Group 1 in pink color cluster, This group belong where annual income is between 60-120, age is between 20 - 45 and spending score is between 20-40, Example: In a shopping mall this group can be targetted with latest technology at affordable price as this group belongs to young and middle age people with less spending but a very high annual income.

Group 2 in purple color cluster in the top right side of the graph, his group belong where annual income is between 60-120, age is between 20 - 45 and spending score is between 70-100, Example: In a shopping mall this group can be targetted to sell expensive watches, expensive jewellery, designer clothes as this are people with high annual income with high spending score.

Group 3 in orange color cluster in the bottom right side of the graph, this group belong where annual income is less between 20 - 45 and spending is very high between 80 - 100 and age is between 20 - 40, Example: In a shopping mall this group can be targetted to sell everyday products with new offers and attractiveness, this group have highh spending score but very less annual income, so they can easily buy thinghs which is less expensive and used in everyday life.

Group 4 in maroon color cluster in the bottom left side of the graph, this group belong where annual income is less between 20 - 45, age is less between 20 - 45 and spending score is also very less between 20 - 45. This group is very particular in terms of buying. Example: In a shopping mall this group can be targetted to sell items which are very basic and required in everyday use like in grocery store milk, bread, egg, etc. we can promote some offer like buy 3 carat of egg and you will get bread for 50% of the price.

Group 5 in yellow color cluster in the center, this group contains people with average income and average spending score but they are in the early age between 20 - 40. Example: This people are more into general shopping and they can purchase product with average price and quality

Group 6 in purple color in the center, this group contains people with average income and average spending score but they are in the late age 40 - 80 years. Example: In a shopping mall this group can be targetted to sell items like medicine, health related products, some offers in wheel chair or similar items used during old age.

According to my own intuition by looking at the above graph I gave some example and aggregated them into 6 different categories

Then after getting the results we can accordingly make different marketing strategies and policies to optimize the spending scores of the customer in the Mall.
